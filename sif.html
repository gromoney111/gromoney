Specialised Investment Fund (SIF)

SEBI's newest investment category — bridging the gap between mutual funds and PMS. Concentrated portfolios, derivatives strategies & long-short for investors with ₹10 Lakh+.

What is SIF (Specialised Investment Fund)?

The Specialised Investment Fund (SIF) is a new investment category introduced by SEBI in 2024-25 that sits between regular mutual funds and Portfolio Management Services (PMS). It allows AMCs to offer concentrated, high-conviction portfolios and advanced strategies (derivatives, long-short) to investors with a minimum of ₹10 Lakhs.

SIF was created to make PMS-like strategies accessible to a wider investor base. Think of it as "PMS democratised through the mutual fund structure."

SIF is SEBI's answer to the gap between ₹500 SIP mutual funds and ₹50 Lakh PMS — giving HNIs access to concentrated, strategy-driven portfolios at just ₹10 Lakh minimum.

Key Features of SIF

  • Concentrated Alpha — 10-20 stocks for maximum impact per pick
  • Derivatives Strategies — Options for income generation or hedging
  • Long-Short — Can short overvalued stocks + go long on undervalued
  • Lower Entry vs PMS — ₹10L minimum (vs ₹50L for PMS)
  • SEBI Mutual Fund Framework — Full regulatory oversight and transparency
  • NAV-Based — Daily NAV like mutual funds for easy tracking
  • Professional Management — Same star fund managers with enhanced flexibility

Types of SIF Strategies

  • Concentrated Equity — 10-20 high-conviction stocks for maximum alpha
  • Long-Short Equity — Buy undervalued + short overvalued for market-neutral returns
  • Derivatives-Enhanced — Options strategies for income generation or hedging
  • Sector Rotation — Active sector switching based on macro cycles
  • Momentum/Factor — Quantitative strategy exploiting price momentum
  • Multi-Asset Active — Dynamic allocation across equity, debt, gold, international

Who Should Invest in SIF?

  • Investors with ₹10 Lakhs - ₹50 Lakhs seeking higher returns than regular mutual funds
  • Experienced MF investors wanting concentrated, high-conviction portfolios
  • HNIs who find PMS minimum (₹50L) too high but want PMS-like strategies
  • Investors comfortable with higher volatility for potentially higher returns
  • Those seeking derivatives-based income strategies or hedged portfolios
