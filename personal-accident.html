Personal Accident Insurance

Financial protection against death, disability and medical expenses caused by accidents — 24x7, anywhere in the world.

What is Personal Accident Insurance?

Personal Accident (PA) Insurance is a policy that provides financial compensation if the insured person suffers bodily injury, disability or death solely due to an accident. Unlike health insurance which covers hospitalisation from illness, PA insurance specifically covers accident-related events — whether at home, workplace, on the road or while travelling abroad.

A PA policy pays a lump-sum benefit upon accidental death or permanent disability, and reimburses medical expenses incurred due to accidental injuries. It acts as a financial safety net ensuring your family's lifestyle and obligations are not disrupted by an unforeseen accident.

Key Features

  • Accidental Death Benefit: Lump-sum payment (100% of Sum Insured) to the nominee if the policyholder dies due to an accident.
  • Permanent Total Disability: Full sum insured paid if the insured suffers permanent total disability (e.g., loss of both limbs, both eyes) due to an accident.
  • Permanent Partial Disability: Percentage of sum insured paid based on the degree of disability as per the policy schedule.
  • Temporary Total Disability: Weekly compensation paid if the insured is temporarily unable to work due to accidental injury.
  • Medical Expenses Reimbursement: Covers hospitalisation, surgery, medicines and treatment costs arising from an accident.
  • Transportation of Mortal Remains: Covers cost of transporting the body to hometown in case of accidental death away from home.
  • Children's Education Benefit: Some plans offer additional benefit for children's education if the earning parent dies in an accident.

Benefits of Personal Accident Insurance

  • 24x7 Worldwide Coverage: You are covered round the clock — at home, at work, while commuting, travelling domestically or internationally. Accidents don't follow a schedule, and neither does your PA cover.
  • Affordable Premiums: PA insurance is one of the most cost-effective insurance products available. You can get a cover of ₹25 lakh to ₹1 crore for as little as ₹500–₹3,000 per year.
  • Tax Benefits under Section 80D: Premiums paid towards personal accident insurance qualify for tax deduction under Section 80D of the Income Tax Act, helping you save up to ₹25,000 (₹50,000 for senior citizens) in taxable income.
  • Cashless Treatment: Many insurers offer cashless hospitalisation at network hospitals for accident-related admissions, so you don't need to arrange funds during emergencies.
  • No Medical Check-up Required: Most PA policies do not require any medical examination at the time of purchase, making it quick and hassle-free to get covered.
  • Covers All Types of Accidents: Road accidents, falls, burns, drowning, animal bites, poisoning, electrocution — all accidental bodily injuries are covered.
  • Complements Health & Life Insurance: PA insurance fills the gap that health and life insurance leave — providing disability income and accident-specific benefits that other policies don't cover.
  • Family Plans Available: Cover your entire family (spouse + children) under a single policy at a nominal additional premium.

Who Should Buy Personal Accident Insurance?

  • Sole Breadwinners: If your family depends on your income, a PA policy ensures they receive financial support if you are disabled or killed in an accident.
  • Frequent Travellers: Those who commute daily, travel frequently for work, or go on road trips face higher accident risk.
  • Two-wheeler & Car Owners: Road accidents are a leading cause of accidental death in India. PA insurance provides coverage beyond the basic motor third-party cover.
  • Self-employed & Freelancers: Without employer-provided accident cover or paid sick leave, self-employed individuals need PA insurance for income protection.
  • Outdoor & Adventure Enthusiasts: If you enjoy trekking, cycling, sports or any adventure activities, PA cover gives you peace of mind.
  • Senior Citizens: Elderly people are more prone to falls and fractures. A PA policy covers treatment costs and provides disability benefits.
  • Everyone aged 18–65: Accidents are unpredictable. Regardless of your occupation or lifestyle, a PA policy is a smart, low-cost addition to your insurance portfolio.
